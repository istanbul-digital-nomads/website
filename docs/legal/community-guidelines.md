## What Istanbul Nomads is

We're a community for people working remotely from Istanbul - nomads passing
through, remote workers settling in, local guides who know the city, tour
guides with credentials, and agents who help with visas and paperwork. We
exist to help you meet each other in real life, get the most out of the
city, and feel less alone here.

These guidelines exist so that **everyone here can trust everyone else**.
Read them once. Honor them every day.

---

## The five rules

If you can't read the rest of this page right now, read these.

1. **Show up. If you can't, say so.** RSVPing and ghosting is the single
   most damaging behavior in this community. Cancel by 24h before the plan
   starts and you owe nobody anything. No-show without a message and you
   get a strike.
2. **Be honest about what you're offering.** Hosts describe their plan
   accurately - vibe, pace, language, cost, who it's for. If it's a quiet
   coworking session, don't bring loud guests. If it's a 12km hike, don't
   undersell it as "a walk."
3. **Verify before you trust.** Blue and Gold badges mean a real person did
   real verification. Red badge means the basics. Look at the badge before
   you hand someone money or get in a car with them.
4. **Telegram is the messaging layer.** All chat happens on Telegram. We
   don't run DMs on the site on purpose. Don't ask for someone's number,
   Instagram, or address until you've actually met.
5. **Don't make this place weird.** No harassment, no racism, no creeping,
   no proselytizing, no sales pitches dressed as plans. One strike for
   weirdness, two for repetition, three and you're out.

## What every member agrees to

By creating an account, you agree to all of this:

### Honesty

- Use your real first name. A nickname is fine, an alias is not. Your
  profile should reflect a person we could find on Telegram tonight.
- Don't claim a role you don't have. Calling yourself a `local_guide` when
  you've been here three weeks isn't OK. Calling yourself a `tour_guide`
  when you're not licensed is not just dishonest, it's illegal under
  Turkish tourism law.
- Don't fake a verification badge. Don't photoshop a screenshot. Don't
  borrow someone else's ID. We will revoke your account permanently and we
  will tell the community we did.

### Respect

- Don't harass other members. Stalking, repeated unwanted contact, sending
  unsolicited explicit material, doxxing, or threats of any kind get you
  permanently banned.
- Don't discriminate based on race, ethnicity, nationality, religion,
  gender, sexuality, disability, age, or anything else that isn't behavior.
  We don't care where you're from; we care how you treat people.
- Don't proselytize. Hosting a "coffee chat" that turns out to be a
  recruiting pitch for your faith, your MLM, your crypto coin, or your
  political movement is a fast way to lose your account.

### Plans

- If you host a plan, you're the host. You show up first, you're reachable,
  you handle the practical questions, you make sure your attendees feel
  welcome.
- If you join a plan, you respect the host. They told you to meet at Karga
  at 6pm; you're there at 6pm. They said "no laptops, this is a social
  hang"; your laptop stays in your bag.
- If you can't make it, **cancel through the platform at least 24h before
  the start time.** Earlier is better.
- Don't ghost. A late cancellation is forgivable. A no-show is a strike.
  Three strikes in 90 days suspends your account for 30 days; six in 90
  days is permanent.
- Hosts can mark you as no-show after the plan. You can dispute it. If you
  dispute and we find the host was honest, the strike doubles.

### Money

- Only Blue or Gold verified `local_guide`, `tour_guide`, and `agent`
  members can charge entry fees.
- All paid plans go through the platform. Don't ask attendees to pay you
  off-platform, don't accept cash for a plan that was supposed to be
  ticketed, don't quote one price on the platform and charge a different
  one in person. We hold money in escrow for a reason; bypassing it
  bypasses every protection your attendees have.
- For budget plans, the budget field is informational. Hosts can suggest
  "expect to spend 200-400 TL on food and drinks." Hosts cannot then ask
  attendees to Venmo them part of the bill.
- No commission scraping. Don't run a plan whose actual purpose is to send
  attendees to a partner business that pays you a kickback.

### Safety

- Plans involving alcohol, water, mountains, or vehicles carry real risk.
  Read the per-vibe disclaimer on each plan before you join. If the host
  says "this is a moderate hike, you need decent shoes and 2L of water,"
  believe them.
- If you're hosting an `outdoor` plan, you're responsible for telling
  attendees what to bring, what the difficulty is, and what to do if they
  can't keep up. We expect hosts of outdoor plans with real terrain to
  carry basic first aid and to share an emergency contact.
- If you feel unsafe at a plan, leave. Tell us afterward and we'll handle
  it. We will not require you to confront anyone.
- Underage attendees: no plan involving alcohol is appropriate for anyone
  under 18, even if alcohol is in Turkey legal at 18. Hosts must enforce
  this.

### Verification

- Red-badge members get the basics: post free budget plans, join other
  members' plans, comment, RSVP.
- Blue-badge members have passed ID + selfie verification. They can host
  paid plans up to their subscription tier's monthly cap.
- Gold-badge members have met an organizer in person. They get a stronger
  presentation on the site and priority placement in `/today` when
  filtering by host quality.
- Lying on verification is the fastest path to a permanent ban. We keep
  records.

### Content

- Don't post other people's photos without permission, especially not at
  recognizable spaces or homes.
- Don't post copyrighted material as if it were yours.
- Don't post in a language no one in the audience reads. Plans default to
  English. Add Turkish, Persian, Arabic, or Russian as a *secondary* if
  your plan is locale-specific.

## How moderation works

We're not Reddit. There is no algorithm. Moderation is human, transparent,
and slow on purpose.

- **Strikes** are recorded against your account. We tell you when you get
  one and why. You can read your strike history in `/dashboard`.
- **Suspensions** prevent posting and RSVPing but keep your account
  readable. Typical: 7 days for a first strike, 30 days for a second
  within 90 days.
- **Permanent removal** is reserved for: harassment, fraud, ID
  falsification, threats, or behavior that puts other members at physical
  risk.
- **Appeals** go to legal@istanbulnomads.com. We respond within 7 days.
  Decisions are reviewed by an organizer who didn't make the original
  ruling.
- **Doxxing in reverse:** if someone harasses you, we keep your report
  confidential. We do not reveal who reported whom.

Organizers have the final call. We try to be fair and we usually are; if
you think we got it wrong, the appeal address above is the right way to
say so.

## What we won't do

- We won't run public shaming. If you're banned, we tell the community a
  member was removed for a category of behavior; we don't name names.
- We won't surface engagement metrics on plans (likes, follows, "trending
  plans"). The board is chronological for a reason.
- We won't sell your data. Ever. See the [Privacy Policy](privacy.md) for
  the long version.

---

## How to report something

- **Inside the platform:** every member profile and plan card has a "Report"
  link. Use it. Tell us what happened in your own words.
- **Email:** report@istanbulnomads.com - reaches an organizer within 24h
  on weekdays, 48h on weekends.
- **Telegram:** if you're already in the community Telegram, message any
  organizer directly. We have a private channel for safety reports.
- **Emergency:** if someone is in immediate danger, call 112 (Turkish
  emergency line) first, then tell us afterward.

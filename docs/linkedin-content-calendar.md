## 0. What works on LinkedIn in 2026 (evidence-backed)

This section is the source of truth for every format, hook, and timing call below. Every claim cites a 2026 (or late-2025) source. If the evidence shifts, this section gets updated first, and the calendar gets re-balanced against it.

### Format hierarchy (the single biggest lever)

Carousel / native document posts are the dominant format in 2026 by a wide margin.

- Native documents hit a **7.00% average engagement rate**, the top of all formats, with a 14% year-over-year jump. Multi-image posts land at 6.60%, text at roughly 4%, polls at 4.40%. Source: [Social Insider - LinkedIn Organic Benchmarks 2026](https://www.socialinsider.io/social-media-benchmarks/linkedin) and [Meet-Lea - LinkedIn Engagement Metrics 2026](https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-engagement-metrics-benchmarks).
- One 2026 benchmark puts carousel + document engagement at **3.7x higher than text-only posts**. Source: [CarouselMaker - Carousels vs Text vs Videos 2026](https://carouselmaker.co/en/blog/linkedin-carousels-vs-text-posts-vs-videos).
- Carousel posts have **45% higher dwell time and 22% more saves than plain text**. Source: [Adobe Express - LinkedIn saves and shares](https://www.adobe.com/express/learn/blog/linkedin-saves-shares).
- Counterweight: mixed-format accounts get 37% more follower growth than single-format accounts, so don't only post carousels. Source: [CarouselMaker](https://carouselmaker.co/en/blog/linkedin-carousels-vs-text-posts-vs-videos).

Implication for us: rebalance the 13-post calendar toward carousels / documents. Old mix was 3 carousels / 9 text / 1 poll. New mix is 5 carousels-or-documents / 6 text / 1 poll / 1 comment-starter.

### Algorithm signals that matter in 2026

LinkedIn's primary ranking signal is the **Depth Score**, a composite dominated by dwell time and comment depth. Source: [Linkboost - LinkedIn Algorithm Changes 2026](https://blog.linkboost.co/linkedin-algorithm-changes-2026/).

- Dwell time is measured in seconds; carousels inflate it because every swipe counts as active engagement. Source: [Linkboost](https://blog.linkboost.co/linkedin-algorithm-changes-2026/).
- Comment depth matters more than comment count. Multi-reply threads and longer comments carry more weight than a wave of one-liners. Source: [Linkboost](https://blog.linkboost.co/linkedin-algorithm-changes-2026/).
- The initial distribution window widened from ~90 minutes to a 3-8 hour "Momentum Model," so a post with slow but sustained engagement can keep picking up reach all day. Source: [Linkboost](https://blog.linkboost.co/linkedin-algorithm-changes-2026/).
- **Saves are roughly 5x more valuable than a like** as an algorithm signal. Source: [Adobe Express](https://www.adobe.com/express/learn/blog/linkedin-saves-shares).

Implication: we reply in-thread to every early comment to farm depth, not just hearts. We design every post to be saveable (a specific checklist, a price, a coordinate, a step).

### Hooks (the above-the-fold is still everything)

- On mobile, ~140 characters appear before "see more"; on desktop, ~210. Source: [Socialrails - Character Limits 2026](https://socialrails.com/blog/linkedin-post-character-limits).
- "How I..." personal-story posts get **3x more saves than listicles**, and personal-story posts beat promotional posts by 38% in engagement. Source: [Adobe Express](https://www.adobe.com/express/learn/blog/linkedin-saves-shares).
- Winning 2026 hooks sound like thoughts, not headlines. Tension, specific counterintuitive claims, and list promises with a number outperform rhetorical questions and "In today's fast-paced world..." openers. Source: [Medium - LinkedIn Hooks That Actually Work in 2026](https://medium.com/@viralboris/linkedin-hooks-that-actually-work-in-2026-50-examples-bbe7976cde67).

Implication: every first-person "I did this, here's what I learned" hook in our calendar stays. We rewrote two hooks that skewed listicle-flat into first-person story form (see Posts 6, 12).

### Length, line breaks, emoji

- Sweet spot for text posts is **1,301-2,500 characters** for top engagement, though 100-300 characters can spike engagement rate if the whole post fits above the fold. Source: [Socialrails](https://socialrails.com/blog/linkedin-post-character-limits).
- Paragraphs of 1-2 sentences, lots of whitespace, mobile-first formatting. Source: [Socialrails](https://socialrails.com/blog/linkedin-post-character-limits).
- Emoji help as visual signposts but should be minimal. One to two max. Source: [SalesRobot - Emojis on LinkedIn 2026](https://www.salesrobot.co/blogs/linkedin-emojis).

We already follow all three. Nothing to change.

### Hashtags in 2026

LinkedIn removed hashtag-following in late 2024 and now uses its "360Brew" 150B-parameter AI to categorize posts. Hashtags are classification metadata, not discovery channels. Source: [Media Mister - LinkedIn Hashtags 2026](https://www.mediamister.com/blog/linkedin-hashtags/).

- **3-5 hashtags** is the current sweet spot. Posts with 6+ see lower engagement. Source: [ConnectSafely - LinkedIn Hashtags 2026](https://connectsafely.ai/articles/linkedin-hashtags).
- Targeted posts (1-3 tags) see a **12.6% higher engagement rate** than posts with none. Source: [ConnectSafely](https://connectsafely.ai/articles/linkedin-hashtags).
- Hashtags in the post body outweigh hashtags in comments. Broad + niche + branded is the working formula. Source: [Media Mister](https://www.mediamister.com/blog/linkedin-hashtags/).

We already use 3-5. Keep.

### Posting time for a global English-speaking nomad audience

- Tuesday-Thursday, 8-11 AM in the audience's local timezone. Wednesday is the single strongest day. Weekends are nearly dead. Source: [Sprout Social - Best Times 2026](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/best-times-to-post-on-linkedin/) and [Postiv - Best Time to Post 2026](https://postiv.ai/blog/best-time-to-post-on-linkedin).
- For a global audience, 9 AM in a major timezone (EST or GMT) captures European morning and US morning in one slot. Source: [LiSeller - LinkedIn Global Reach](https://www.liseller.com/linkedin-growth-blog/best-times-to-post-on-linkedin-for-global-reach).

Implication for Istanbul (TRT = GMT+3): our strongest global slot is **09:00-10:30 TRT** (6-7:30 AM GMT, 7-8:30 AM CET, catches Europe waking up). **14:00-15:00 TRT** (7-8 AM EST) is the "catch New York waking up" slot for US-focused posts like the country playbooks and the big comparison carousel. Friday 12:30 stays for the slow, conversational Istanbul-heavy audience but drops to second-best.

### Topics that drive saves for this audience

- Tactical "give away the recipe" posts dominate saves. Source: [PostFast - Complete Guide 2026](https://postfa.st/blog/complete-guide-to-succeeding-on-linkedin-in-2026).
- Checklists near the end of posts measurably increase save rate. Source: [Adobe Express](https://www.adobe.com/express/learn/blog/linkedin-saves-shares).
- For the nomad/remote-work audience specifically, **"slowmading"** (long stays, routines, building businesses in one base) is the dominant 2026 narrative and aligns exactly with our positioning. Source: [Yahoo Travel - 8 Cities Nomads Are Moving To in 2026](https://travel.yahoo.com/advice/lifestyle/articles/8-cities-digital-nomads-creators-223741352.html).

Implication: we're on the right side of the current narrative. Lean into "slowmad" language in Posts 5, 11, 12.

### LinkedIn newsletter benchmarks

LinkedIn newsletters still skew smaller in total reach but deliver dramatically higher dwell time and save rates per subscriber because they land in inbox + notification, unlike feed posts. The 2026 format benchmarks don't isolate newsletters cleanly, but [Social Media Today's reporting on document posts](https://www.socialmediatoday.com/news/report-shows-document-posts-on-linkedin-see-more-engagement/816551/) shows that long-form saveable content (including newsletters and documents) compounds faster than short-form text.

Implication: we don't launch a newsletter in month 1. Month 2 we test one newsletter issue built from the Path-to-Istanbul playbook that performed best.

## 3. Zero-Follower Launch Tactics (Week 1-2)

Nobody will see the posts in the feed. Distribution has to be manufactured.

**Comment strategy (the real growth lever).** Every day, leave 3 substantive comments (40+ words, a specific opinion or correction) on posts by adjacent creators. Target list to build manually week 1:
- Remote-work creators writing about Lisbon, Bali, Mexico City, Tbilisi
- Istanbul-based founders and tech ops people
- Digital nomad visa commentators (Turkey, Portugal, Spain schemes)
- Relocation consultants (Iranian, Russian, Nigerian diaspora voices)

No drive-by "Great post!". Comment with a lived-in Istanbul counterpoint or a specific addition. The LinkedIn profile click-through from good comments beats cold posting at zero followers.

**Employee / founder advocacy.** Every post shared by the page must be reshared-with-commentary (not just "reposted") by the founder's personal profile within 30 minutes. Personal profiles outreach 10-20x a brand page at low follower counts. Ask 2-3 close community members to do the same for the first 10 posts.

**Hashtag targeting (narrow beats broad).** At 0 followers, `#DigitalNomads` buries you under 20k daily posts. Use it sparingly. Lean on mid-tail tags: `#IstanbulLife`, `#RemoteWorkEurope`, `#SlowmadLife`, `#TurkeyNomad`, `#KadikoyLife`. One broad, two medium, one narrow per post.

**Groups to engage in (not spam).** Comment for two weeks before posting: "Digital Nomads Around the World", "Remote Work & Jobs", "Expats in Istanbul", "Turkey Nomads" (check for active ones). Also Slack/Discord: Nomad List, Freaking Nomads. Not for link drops - for reputation.

**DM outreach template (for first 25 hand-picked contacts).**

> Hi [name] - saw your post about [specific detail from their post]. I run istanbulnomads.com, a small community + guide for nomads living in Istanbul. Not pitching anything, just wanted to connect with someone else thinking about [their topic]. If you're ever passing through Istanbul, happy to share the cafe list we actually use.

Three to four sentences, a real reason to talk, no ask. Sent from the founder's personal profile, never the company page.

**Launch amplification.** The first 5 posts: tag no one invented, but reshare each onto the founder's personal feed same day with one added sentence of context. The page follows grow from personal-profile visitors, not the other way around.

## 11. AI Image Generation Guide

Every post below has an **Image prompt (Nano Banana / MJ / Flux)** line. Every one of those prompts assumes the master style card from `docs/visual-identity.md` is already active in the generation session. Do not generate any image without pasting it first.

> **See `docs/visual-identity.md` for the master style card, palette lock, scene archetypes, carousel consistency protocol, hard nevers, negative prompts, and the QA checklist.**

This section keeps only the LinkedIn-specific bits: tool pick rationale, aspect cheat sheet, and the carousel recipe pointer.

### Tool selection

- **Nano Banana (Google Gemini 2.5 Flash Image)** is the default. Best at photorealistic Istanbul scenes, strong at preserving a reference image's palette across a multi-image chain (perfect for carousel consistency). Use for: all photoreal text-post imagery, all carousel slide backgrounds, all space spotlights, ferry / Bosphorus / cafe scenes. Weaknesses: hands, dense text in-image.
- **Midjourney v7** is the choice when you want heightened mood, painterly light, or a more editorial documentary feel (Posts 5, 11, 13). Weaker at exact repeatability across a carousel, stronger at a single hero shot.
- **Flux 1.1 Pro** is the choice when Nano Banana rejects the prompt or you need a more literal interpretation (map flatlays, document-style imagery for Post 3).
- **Canva / Figma (no AI)** for the carousel itself: layout, typography, the title card. AI generates the background plate or the central photo only. Text goes on in Canva so you can iterate copy without regenerating.
- **DALL-E 3** only for quick one-off illustrations when you need a whimsical editorial motif (not used in this calendar).

### Master style card, palette, negatives, carousel recipe, text-on-image rule

See `docs/visual-identity.md`. That doc owns all of the above. When it changes, every image regenerated after the change uses the new version. Do not duplicate the style card here.

## Why this list matters

Every month, new nomads join our [Telegram group](https://t.me/istanbul_digital_nomads) and ask the same questions. We love answering them. But the best version of those answers is this: the mistakes we all made, condensed into a list you can read on the plane.

These come from real community members. Every single one cost someone time, money, or unnecessary stress.

## 1. I booked a month-long Airbnb without visiting the neighborhood first

**The mistake:** Committing to a full month in Taksim because it "looked central on the map." Taksim is loud, touristy, and not where nomads work. A month of poor sleep and overpriced cafes.

**What to do instead:** Book 3-4 nights in a hotel or short Airbnb. Use those days to walk [Kadikoy, Cihangir, and Besiktas](/guides/neighborhoods) in person. Then book your month.

## 2. I bought a SIM card at the airport

**The mistake:** Paying 1,200 TL for a Vodafone tourist SIM at the Istanbul Airport counter. The same Turkcell plan costs 250 TL at a city store.

**What to do instead:** Use airport wifi to message your contacts, then buy a [Turkcell SIM](/guides/internet) at a company-owned store in Kadikoy or Taksim the next day. Bring your passport.

## 3. I took a taxi from the airport without BiTaksi

**The mistake:** A taxi driver at the arrivals exit offered a "flat rate" of 1,500 TL to Kadikoy. The metered fare should have been 600-700 TL.

**What to do instead:** Download [BiTaksi](https://www.bitaksi.com/en) before landing. It shows the estimated fare and tracks the route. Or take the [Havaist bus](/guides/transport) for 150 TL.

## 4. I did not get an Istanbulkart on day one

**The mistake:** Paying cash for a taxi to every destination for two days because "I will figure out the metro later." Spent 800 TL on taxis that would have cost 100 TL on public transport.

**What to do instead:** Buy an [Istanbulkart](/guides/transport) at the first metro station or Migros supermarket you see. It costs 70 TL and works on everything - metro, bus, tram, ferry.

## 5. I tried to work from a random cafe without testing the wifi first

**The mistake:** Ordering a full breakfast at a beautiful Cihangir cafe, opening my laptop, and discovering the wifi was 2 Mbps with constant drops. Lost a morning of work.

**What to do instead:** Open [fast.com](https://fast.com) on your phone before sitting down. Our [coworking guide](/guides/coworking) lists 15+ cafes with verified reliable wifi.

## 6. I ate only in the tourist area

**The mistake:** Spending 400-600 TL per meal near Sultanahmet and the Grand Bazaar for the first week. Generic "Turkish food" at tourist markup.

**What to do instead:** Eat at a [lokanta](/guides/food) in any residential neighborhood. A full meal with soup, main, salad, and bread costs 150-250 TL. The food is better, too.

## 7. I did not learn any Turkish

**The mistake:** Assuming English would work everywhere. It does in nomad-friendly cafes. It does not at the grocery store, the post office, or the immigration office.

**What to do instead:** Learn [10-20 basic phrases](/guides/culture) before arriving. "Tesekkur ederim" (thank you), "Hesap lutfen" (check please), and "Ne kadar?" (how much?) will cover 80% of daily interactions.

## 8. I ignored the Asian side completely

**The mistake:** Staying on the European side for three weeks because "that is where everything is." Missed Kadikoy entirely, which is where most of the nomad community actually lives.

**What to do instead:** Take the [ferry to Kadikoy](/blog/ferry-commute-guide) on day two. Walk the market streets. Visit MOB coworking. You might never go back to the European side for work.

## 9. I did not register my address

**The mistake:** Not telling my landlord to register my address with the local authorities. When I applied for the [residence permit](/blog/getting-residence-permit), the immigration office said my address was not registered and I had to come back.

**What to do instead:** Ask your landlord to complete the address registration (nufus mudurlugu) within the first week. It is their legal obligation. Read more in our [housing guide](/guides/housing).

## 10. I exchanged money at the airport

**The mistake:** Exchanged $500 at the airport currency desk. The exchange rate was 8% worse than the market rate. Lost about $40 immediately.

**What to do instead:** Use a [Wise or Revolut card](/guides/cost-of-living) for all transactions. They give the real exchange rate. Withdraw TL from ATMs only if needed, and decline the ATM's "conversion offer."

## 11. I did not join the community early enough

**The mistake:** Spending three weeks alone, working from random cafes, eating solo. Joined the Telegram group in week four and immediately got invited to a coworking day, a rooftop meetup, and a walking tour.

**What to do instead:** Join the [Telegram group](https://t.me/istanbul_digital_nomads) before you arrive. Introduce yourself. Come to the next event. The community exists to make your first month easier.

## 12. I overpacked

**The mistake:** Brought two large suitcases because "what if I need it." Istanbul has everything. Good brands, cheap markets, even an IKEA for apartment basics.

**What to do instead:** One carry-on and one backpack. Buy what you need locally. Turkish sizes run similar to European. The [weekly pazar markets](/guides/food) sell household items cheaply.

## The meta-lesson

Most of these mistakes share a root cause: treating Istanbul like a vacation destination instead of a place to build a routine. The faster you switch from tourist mode to resident mode, the better your experience gets.

Read the [guides](/guides) before you arrive. Join the [community](https://t.me/istanbul_digital_nomads) before you land. And give the city more than a week before forming opinions.

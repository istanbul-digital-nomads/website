## Why I am writing this

The official Turkish immigration website tells you what documents you need. It doesn't tell you what the appointment is actually like, how long things take in practice, or what surprises to expect. This is the guide I wish I had.

**My situation:** EU passport, entering on a tourist e-visa, applying for a short-term residence permit (ikamet) from inside Turkey. I applied in Kadikoy.

## Timeline overview

| Step | When | Time taken |
|---|---|---|
| Applied online | Day 45 of tourist visa | 30 minutes |
| Got appointment date | 3 days later | - |
| Attended appointment | Day 55 | 2 hours |
| Received ikamet card | Day 82 (27 days after appointment) | - |

Total time from application to card in hand: **37 days**.

## Step 1: Apply online (e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr)

The online form is straightforward but entirely in Turkish. Use Google Translate. Key fields:

- **Residence type:** Short-term residence permit
- **Reason:** "Tourism" works fine for nomads
- **Address:** Your registered Turkish address (your landlord must have registered you)
- **Duration:** Request 1 year. They may grant 6 months.

> **Important:** Apply at least 3 weeks before your tourist visa expires. Once your application is submitted, you are legally allowed to stay in Turkey while it's processed.

After submitting, you get an appointment date at your local immigration office. Mine was 10 days out.

## Step 2: Prepare documents

This is what I actually brought (and what they checked):

**Required:**
- Passport + photocopy of all stamped pages
- 4 biometric photos (white background, taken at any photo shop for 30 TL)
- Health insurance policy covering Turkey (I used a local insurer - 350 TL for 1 year)
- Lease contract (notarized or with landlord's ID copy)
- Bank statement showing sufficient funds (~$500/month for the requested duration)
- Application form printout from e-ikamet

**They did NOT ask for:**
- Proof of income or employment
- University degree
- Return flight booking

> **Tip:** Get your health insurance from a local Turkish insurer (like Axa Sigorta or Acibadem Sigorta). They cost 200-500 TL/year and are instantly accepted. International policies like SafetyWing sometimes require extra verification.

## Step 3: The appointment

The Kadikoy immigration office is on the Asian side, near the Kadikoy courthouse. I arrived 15 minutes early.

**What happened:**
1. Security check at the entrance
2. Take a number at the reception desk
3. Wait (I waited 45 minutes)
4. Called to a desk where an officer reviewed my documents
5. Fingerprints and photo taken
6. Signed the application
7. Paid the fee (1,200 TL at the time, paid by card)
8. Done. Total time at the office: about 2 hours.

The officer spoke basic English. He asked me two questions: "Where do you live?" and "How long do you want to stay?" That was it.

## Step 4: Wait for the card

After the appointment, you get a temporary document (a paper printout) that proves your application is being processed. This is your legal right to stay.

The actual ikamet card arrived by PTT (Turkish post) to my registered address 27 days later. Some people get it in 2 weeks, others wait 2 months. You can track the status online.

## Tips from the experience

1. **Book your appointment in Kadikoy or Bakirkoy**, not the main Fatih office. Smaller offices are less crowded and faster.
2. **Bring originals AND photocopies** of everything. They keep the copies.
3. **Dress normally.** It's a government office but not formal. Casual is fine.
4. **Be patient.** Turkish bureaucracy moves at its own pace. Getting frustrated doesn't help.
5. **Ask the community.** Requirements can change without notice. Before your appointment, ask in the [Telegram group](https://t.me/istanbul_digital_nomads) if anyone has been recently.

## The digital nomad visa alternative

If you earn $3,000+/month and have a university degree, the [digital nomad visa](/guides/visa) gives you 12 months directly and might be simpler than the ikamet process. I went with ikamet because I did not meet the income threshold at the time.

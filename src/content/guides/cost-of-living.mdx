## What does a month in Istanbul actually cost?

Istanbul is affordable compared to Western Europe, but it is not cheap by Turkish standards. Prices have risen significantly due to inflation (around 30% annually). The good news: if you earn in USD, EUR, or GBP, your money goes a long way.

**Quick answer:** A single nomad spending carefully can live well on **$1,000-$1,500/month**. A comfortable lifestyle with a nice apartment and regular dining out runs **$1,500-$2,200/month**.

## Three monthly budgets

### Budget lifestyle (~$1,000/month)

You are sharing an apartment or renting a studio outside the center, cooking most meals at home, using public transport, and working from cafes.

| Category | Monthly (TL) | Monthly (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Rent (studio, outer neighborhood) | 12,000 TL | ~$385 |
| Groceries | 5,000 TL | ~$160 |
| Eating out (occasional) | 2,000 TL | ~$65 |
| Transport (Istanbulkart) | 500 TL | ~$16 |
| Phone/internet | 300 TL | ~$10 |
| Cafes (coffee while working) | 1,500 TL | ~$48 |
| Entertainment/misc | 2,000 TL | ~$65 |
| **Total** | **~23,300 TL** | **~$750** |

> This is tight but doable. You will be further from the center and eating at home most days.

### Moderate lifestyle (~$1,500/month)

You have a furnished 1-bedroom in Kadikoy or a similar neighborhood, eat out a few times a week, and have a coworking membership.

| Groceries | 6,000 TL | ~$190 |

| Transport (Istanbulkart + occasional taxi) | 1,000 TL | ~$32 |

> This is the sweet spot for most nomads. Comfortable without overspending.

## Specific prices to know

### Food and drink

| Item | Price (TL) | Price (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Simit (street bread) | 15-20 TL | ~$0.50 |
| Doner/kebab wrap | 80-150 TL | ~$3-5 |
| Meal at a local restaurant | 200-400 TL | ~$6-13 |
| Meal at a mid-range restaurant | 500-800 TL | ~$16-26 |
| Cappuccino (cafe) | 80-120 TL | ~$3-4 |
| Beer (bar) | 120-200 TL | ~$4-6 |
| Grocery bag (weekly basics) | 1,000-1,500 TL | ~$32-48 |
| Water (1.5L bottle) | 15-25 TL | ~$0.50-0.80 |

> **Tip:** Eat where locals eat. Look for "lokanta" (cafeteria-style) restaurants where a full meal with soup, main course, and salad costs 150-250 TL (~$5-8). They are everywhere and the food is excellent.

### Transport

| Item | Price (TL) | Price (USD) |
|---|---|---|
| Istanbulkart (reloadable card) | 70 TL (card cost) | ~$2.25 |
| Single metro/bus/tram ride | 10-15 TL | ~$0.35-0.50 |
| Monthly transport pass | 500 TL | ~$16 |
| Ferry (Kadikoy to Eminonu) | 10-15 TL | ~$0.35-0.50 |
| Taxi (short ride, 5 km) | 100-150 TL | ~$3-5 |
| Taxi (airport to Kadikoy) | 600-800 TL | ~$19-26 |

> **Tip:** Use the [BiTaksi](https://www.bitaksi.com/en) app instead of hailing taxis on the street. It shows the fare upfront and prevents meter scams.

## Money tips

1. **Use Wise or Revolut** for currency conversion. Turkish bank ATMs charge 3-5% fees. Wise gives you the real exchange rate.
2. **Pay in TL, not USD.** If a shop offers to charge you in dollars, always decline and pay in Turkish lira. The shop's exchange rate is always worse.
3. **Negotiate rent.** Unlike Western Europe, rent in Istanbul is negotiable. Offer 10-15% below the asking price, especially for longer stays.
4. **Shop at local markets (pazar).** Every neighborhood has a weekly street market with fresh produce at half the supermarket price.
5. **Track the exchange rate.** The TL fluctuates. A good rate month can save you hundreds of dollars.
6. **Keep cash for small shops.** Most restaurants and stores accept cards, but small corner shops and street vendors prefer cash.

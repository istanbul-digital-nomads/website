## Do you need a coworking space?

Many nomads in Istanbul work from cafes most of the time. Istanbul's cafe culture is strong, wifi is generally reliable, and nobody rushes you out. But a coworking space gives you a guaranteed fast connection, a proper desk, and a community of people working on similar things.

**Our take:** Get a coworking membership if you take video calls regularly or need reliable 100+ Mbps. Work from cafes if you mostly do deep work and can handle occasional wifi drops.

## Coworking spaces

The most popular coworking chain among Istanbul's nomad community. Multiple locations, modern interiors, and a social atmosphere. The Levent location has a rooftop terrace between skyscrapers. The Karakoy location is closest to the tourist center.

## Best laptop-friendly cafes

If you prefer cafes over coworking spaces, these are the most reliable spots for working. All have good wifi, power outlets, and a laptop-friendly atmosphere.

- **Walter's Coffee Roastery** - Breaking Bad themed, solid wifi, popular with nomads. [Map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Walters+Coffee+Roastery+Kadikoy+Istanbul)

## Tips for working from cafes

1. **Test wifi before ordering.** Open [fast.com](https://fast.com) on your phone before committing to a spot.
2. **Bring a power strip.** Outlets exist but are sometimes shared. A small strip lets you charge multiple devices.
3. **Arrive before 10am.** The best seats with outlets fill up fast, especially on weekdays.
4. **Order regularly.** Buy a drink every 1-2 hours. Cafe owners notice, and it keeps you welcome.
5. **Avoid peak lunch hours (12-2pm)** for cafes in business areas - they get packed and noisy.
6. **Kadikoy and Cihangir** have the highest density of laptop-friendly spots. You can cafe-hop all day without walking more than 10 minutes.
